  1. Browse Marketplace CEA (real-time listings)

Client Onboarding, KYC & AML

Objective: Verify the client's identity, structure, and source of funds before enabling any funding, marketplace access, or trading activity.

0.1 Onboarding Request & Pre-Checks

Typical Timeline
T+0 to T+3

Client Action:

Client contacts Nihao (intro / mandate discussion)
    └── Shares basic company profile and target use-case
    └── Receives Nihao onboarding pack + KYC checklist
    └── Confirms willingness to proceed under Nihao policies

Nihao Internal Actions:

Sales / Relationship team logs the opportunity
    └── Compliance performs initial high-level screening (jurisdiction, sector, sanctions lists)
    └── Decision: Proceed to full KYC OR decline / request further clarification
    └── If proceed: Nihao sends secure KYC document upload instructions

0.2 KYC/KYB Documentation Collection

Typical Timeline
T+1 to T+5

KYC / KYB Package (examples):

  • Corporate documents: Certificate of Incorporation, Articles, Register of Directors & Shareholders.
  • Ownership & control: UBO declaration, structure chart, % shareholdings.
  • Identity: Passport / ID for directors and UBOs, proof of address.
  • Business profile: Description of activities, expected trading volumes, counterparties, geographies.
  • Source of funds / wealth: Supporting documents where required (e.g., audited financials, contracts).

0.3 Compliance Review & Risk Assessment

Typical Timeline
T+2 to T+7
Client Risk Tier Typical Checks Indicative Review Time
Standard Risk Document verification, sanctions / PEP screening, basic source-of-funds check. 2–3 business days
Heightened Risk Enhanced due diligence, deeper source-of-funds analysis, additional approvals. 3–5 business days
High / Prohibited Risk Escalation to senior compliance, potential decline of onboarding. Decision as soon as reasonably practical
Outcome of Review: Client is either (a) approved and onboarded with an assigned risk tier, (b) approved with conditions (e.g., limits, additional monitoring), or (c) declined with documented rationale.

0.5 Ongoing Monitoring & Periodic Reviews

Monitoring
Continuous
  • Transaction monitoring: Ongoing review of trading patterns, volumes, and counterparties against the client's profile.
  • Screening updates: Periodic re-screening against sanctions, PEP, and adverse media lists.
  • Periodic KYC refresh: Updated corporate documents and ownership information on a scheduled basis or upon trigger events.
  • Event-driven reviews: Triggered by unusual activity, large deviations from expected volumes, or regulatory requests.
Principle: Nihao maintains a risk-based approach to KYC/AML, balancing client experience with robust regulatory compliance and ongoing monitoring.

Nihao implements multi-layer risk controls to protect clients and ensure market integrity.

2. Anti-Fraud & Sanctions Screening

  • Real-time Sanctions Screening: All transactions screened against OFAC, EU, UN, UK sanctions lists (updated daily)
  • PEP Monitoring: Counterparties and beneficial owners continuously screened against Politically Exposed Persons lists
  • Adverse Media Screening: Automated monitoring for negative news or regulatory actions involving clients or counterparties
  • Transaction Pattern Analysis: Machine learning algorithms detect unusual trading patterns indicative of money laundering or market abuse
  • Velocity Checks: Alerts triggered for unusual volume spikes or rapid account activity

VALIDATION CHECKLIST (Pre-Execution):

1. KYC Status Check
   ├─ Client account status: APPROVED?
   ├─ AML holds expired?
   ├─ Document refresh due?
   └─ Risk tier compliant with transaction?

2. Funds Availability Check
   ├─ Available balance ≥ order amount?
   ├─ No pending transactions conflicting?
   └─ Account currency matches order currency?

3. Counterparty Verification Check
   ├─ Seller/counterparty status: ACTIVE?
   ├─ Counterparty on sanctions list? → REJECT
   ├─ Counterparty tier appropriate? → ALERT if Tier 3
   └─ Recent adverse media? → REVIEW

4. Limit Compliance Check
   ├─ Order size ≤ single order limit?
   ├─ Daily volume after order ≤ daily limit?
   ├─ Account balance after order ≤ max balance?
   └─ Position size ≤ maximum holding?

5. Asset Verification Check
   ├─ CEA/EUA vintage valid?
   ├─ Quantity matches listing?
   ├─ No duplicate/double-spend attempted?
   └─ Registry compliance verified?

RESULT: PASS ALL → EXECUTE | FAIL ANY → REJECT + ALERT
